Managing Wireless Devices

This section covers managing wireless blood meter devices (PTS CardioChek Plus or Cholestech LDX) on the Oxygen system.​

To access this page, click “Devices” on the Dashboard. This will load the ‘Devices’ page.

Any devices currently connected to the server will be displayed on this page. The main view shows the device name and serial number, the device status, and the pool it is assigned to.

Devices Overview​

Any blood meter devices currently connected to the Oxygen system will be displayed on this page.

This main view shows the device name and serial number, the device status, and the pool it is assigned to.

There is also a ‘View’ button, which when clicked launches a pop-up window providing additional information and actions (described in more detail below).

Device Statuses​

On the ‘Devices' page, the status of a device is displayed for quick reference. Here is an overview of the possible device statuses:

• PTS meters are capable of showing 3 statuses: “idle”, “assigned” (i.e. when a meter has a participant assigned to it), and “offline”.

• LDX machines can show 6 statuses: “idle”, “disconnected”, “reconnected”, “assigned”, “running” (i.e. when running a blood test), and “offline”.

Note: CardioChek Plus meters will automatically power off after 30 minutes of inactivity (i.e. 30 minutes since the last blood test). If a PTS meter powers off (either manually or through inactivity), the status will show as ‘"idle" on the ’Devices' page. If this occurs, and you want this to be correctly reflected on the ‘Devices’ page, you will need to manually take the device offline to match the status of the meter.

‘Assigned To User’ Functionality​

This provides the ability to assign a blood meter to a specific screener. This is particularly useful for screening events using a ‘Single Station’ workflow, where a screener collects all data for a participant through the application.

To assign a meter to a screener, follow these steps:

1. On the ‘Devices’ page, click “View” for a meter. This will launch a pop-up window.

2. Click on the ‘Assigned To User’ selector, and select the name of the desired screener. The screener's name will now be displayed in the selector.

3. Click “Close Details”.

→ The screener's assignment to the meter will be reflected on the main ‘Devices' page. Also, the screener's name will be displayed on the PTS meter or LDX machine.

‘Pool’ Functionality​

This provides the ability to assign meters to specific pools (or groups), ranging from 1 through 8. This can assist in organizing and streamlining your blood test workflow during a screening.

To assign meter to a pool, follow these steps:

1. On the ‘Devices’ page, click “View” for a meter. This will launch a pop-up window.

2. First, ensure that the ‘Assigned To User’ selector is showing “General Pool”.

3. Now click on the ‘Pool’ selector, and select a number for the pool where you’d like this device to be assigned.

4. Click “Close Details”.

→ The updated pool assignment will be reflected on the main ‘Devices’ page. Also, the pool number will update on the meter corresponding to the pool selection.

Note: Whichever pool has the most available devices is where the next participant will be assigned.

Taking a Meter Offline​

This provides the ability to manually remove a meter from the available devices that are ready to run blood tests (without having to turn the device off).

To take a meter offline, follow these steps:

1. On the ‘Devices’ page, click “View” for a meter. This will launch a pop-up window.

2. Under ‘Change Pool’, click the “Take Offline” button.

3. Click “Close Details”.

→ The device's “offline” status will be reflected on the main ‘Devices’ page. Also, “Offline” will be displayed on the meter’s LCD screen.

Placing a Meter Online​

This provides the ability to manually make an offline meter available for running blood tests.

To place a meter online, follow these steps:

1. On the ‘Devices’ page, click “View” for a meter. This will launch a pop-up window.

2. Under ‘Change Pool’, click the “Put Online” button.

3. Click “Close Details”.

→ The device's “online” status will be reflected on the main ‘Devices’ page. Also, the meter will display the ‘Assigned To User’ or ‘Pool’ information as seen on the ‘View’ window.

Notes on batteries and the CardioChek Plus meters​

The PTS CardioChek Plus meters are unique in that they operate only on battery power. As such, the following points should be made:

• PTS meters are designed to run optimally with 4 “AA” batteries. However, the meters can function with just 2 “AA” batteries as well. Depending on your preference, you may choose to run a meter using 4 “AA” batteries, or 2 “AA” batteries (while saving 2 batteries for backups).

• When you initiate a blood test on a PTS meter, the meter scans its available power before running the test. If insufficient battery power exists, it will abort the test and “REPLACE BATTERIES” will appear on the screen. In this case, replace the batteries and turn the meter back on again. At this point, you can pick up where you left off (i.e. the participant's name will re-appear on the meter, and you are ready to run the blood test).

Note: If old batteries are removed but not replaced quickly enough with newer batteries, it's possible the PTS meter may reset itself to factory settings. If this happens, you will need to re-associate the meter with your Oxygen system and then power the meter on again (see instructions here). At this point, the participant's name will re-appear on the meter and you are ready to run the blood test.

Notes on Minimum Requirements​

• The minimum requirement for PTS Meters is firmware version 1.05 or higher. The PTS meter's firmware is shown on the screen for a few seconds when you turn it on.

• With regard to wireless dongles for Cholestech LDX machines, minimum requirements do not apply. StatBridge ensures that each wireless dongle shipped meets all requirements to run with the Oxygen system.
